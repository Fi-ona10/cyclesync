# Syncycle Partner Access — Instagram A/B Ad Test
**Date:** 7–8 May 2026  
**Sprint context:** Feature launch validation — Partner Access  
**Author:** Alisa Adamska  

---

## 1. Background

Syncycle is a science-backed, privacy-first cycle intelligence app that translates hormonal cycle data into personalised daily guidance for nutrition, exercise, productivity, and symptom management. In May 2026, the team shipped **Partner Access** — a feature allowing users to share cycle insights with their partners, enabling them to adjust their behaviour (date ideas, communication style, emotional support) based on the user's current cycle phase.

To validate positioning and identify which audience segment resonates most with the feature, a lightweight A/B test was designed and run on Instagram Stories prior to a broader campaign launch.

---

## 2. Hypothesis

Two competing assumptions were tested:

- **H1 (Male targeting):** Men, framed as wanting to "show up better" for their partners, will engage with ads that give them a sense of insider knowledge and practical guidance.
- **H2 (Female targeting):** Women, framed around the emotional labour of repeatedly explaining their cycle to partners, will engage with ads that promise relief and empowerment.

Both ads promoted the same feature (Partner Access) but used different visual language, copy, and audience targeting.

---

## 3. Experiment Design

| Parameter | Ad 1 — Male | Ad 2 — Female |
|---|---|---|
| **Platform** | Instagram Stories | Instagram Stories |
| **Format** | Static image, 9:16 | Static image, 9:16 |
| **Budget** | €12.00 | €12.00 |
| **Audience** | Manual targeting (male) | Custom audience: Partner Access (Female) |
| **Run date** | Completed 8 May 2026 | Completed 7 May 2026 |

### Ad 1 — Male audience

**Headline:** *She's in her luteal phase. You know what that means.*  
**Subtitle:** *Syncycle Partner Access gives you her cycle insights — so you always know the right move.*  
**CTA:** *Be the partner who just gets it. syncycle.app*  
**Visual:** Dark editorial — two hands sharing a phone showing the Partner Access UI, phone screen as the only light source against a near-black background.

### Ad 2 — Female audience

**Headline:** *Every. Single. Month. / Stop explaining. Let us do that.*  
**Subtitle:** *Syncycle Partner Access translates your cycle science into insights the people you love can act on.*  
**Visual:** Split panel — left half (cold, white) shows a long, unsent iMessage attempting to explain her cycle; right half (deep purple) shows the Syncycle Partner Access app with the Syncycle logo.

---

## 4. Results

### Ad performance

| Metric | Ad 1 — Male | Ad 2 — Female |
|---|---|---|
| **Impressions** | 1,016 | 922 |
| **Website visits** | 47 | 52 |
| **Click-through rate (CTR)** | 4.63% | **5.64%** |
| **Spend** | €11.88 | €11.89 |
| **Cost per click (CPC)** | €0.25 | **€0.23** |
| **CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions)** | €11.69 | €12.90 |

**Winner: Ad 2 (Female).** It achieved 22% higher CTR and 8% lower CPC on near-identical spend. Despite generating fewer impressions, it drove more absolute website visits (52 vs 47).

### Website analytics (last 30 days, Google Analytics)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| **Total users** | 97 (100% new) |
| **Sessions** | 189 |
| **Page views** | ~1,000 |
| **Avg. session duration** | 8m 58s |
| **Engagement rate** | 49.7% |
| **Bounce rate** | 50.3% |

Combined, the two ads generated approximately 99 clicks. The website recorded 97 new users — suggesting near-total attribution of site traffic to the ad campaigns during this period, with minimal organic baseline.

The average session duration of **8m 58s** is notably high for cold ad traffic, indicating that visitors who clicked through were meaningfully engaged with the product rather than bouncing immediately after landing.

---

## 5. Interpretation

### What worked

The female-targeted ad outperformed on every efficiency metric. The split-panel format — contrasting the emotional labour of explaining one's cycle with the relief of Syncycle doing it — appears to have resonated strongly with the target segment. The "Every. Single. Month." framing created immediate recognition of a shared frustration, which is a reliable conversion trigger for cold audiences.

The male-targeted ad also performed credibly, with a 4.63% CTR that sits above typical Instagram Stories benchmarks (industry average: ~0.5–1%). The "insider knowledge" framing — treating the man as someone gaining access to information rather than being educated — appears to have been an appropriate positioning choice.

The high session duration across both audiences suggests the landing page (syncycle.app) is well-aligned with ad expectations — visitors did not feel misled and chose to explore the product in depth.

### Limitations

- **Sample size is small.** With ~1,000 impressions per variant and a €12 budget, statistical significance cannot be claimed. This is an indicative signal, not a conclusive result.
- **Single creative per variant.** Only one visual and copy combination was tested per audience. The observed difference could be attributable to the creative format, the copy, the visual style, or the audience segment — these are confounded.
- **No conversion tracking below click.** Website visits are measured, but sign-ups, Partner Access activations, or any downstream conversion events were not captured in this test.
- **Timing overlap.** The two ads completed on consecutive days (7 and 8 May). Any platform-level effects (day-of-week, algorithm changes) could have introduced noise.

---

## 6. Learnings

1. **Female-audience targeting via emotional resonance outperformed male-audience targeting via aspirational empowerment**, at least at this creative and budget level.
2. **A split-panel "before/after" format proved effective** for a feature whose core value proposition is transformation — from explaining to not having to explain.
3. **Ad-driven traffic to syncycle.app is high-quality** (long session duration, ~50% engagement), suggesting the product experience matches ad expectations.
4. **Both audiences are viable.** The male ad's strong absolute CTR suggests it is worth iterating rather than abandoning.

---

## 7. Next Steps

| Action | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Re-run female ad at higher budget | Validate CTR and CPC at scale; establish statistical significance |
| Test a second female creative (editorial bold: "Stop explaining. Let us do it.") | Isolate whether the split format or the copy is driving performance |
| Add conversion event tracking (Partner Access activation) | Move beyond clicks to actual feature adoption as the primary KPI |
| Iterate male creative with warmer, more emotional framing | Test whether empathy-led positioning (vs. competence-led) performs differently |
| A/B test landing page with female-first vs. couple-first hero | Optimise post-click experience to improve the 50.3% bounce rate |
