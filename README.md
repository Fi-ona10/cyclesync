<strong> User survey outcome</strong>
<p>## MAIN SURVEY FINDINGS

**Overall Impression:**

- 55+ responses from women aged 18-40+
- High interest in holistic cycle tracking (80%+)
- Strong demand for personalized, concrete advice beyond generic tips
- Privacy concerns and price sensitivity are major barriers

**Most Common Symptoms:**

- Low energy/Fatigue (70%+)
- PMS symptoms (60%+)
- Digestive issues/bloating (55%+)
- Food cravings (50%+)
- Painful periods (45%+)

## CURRENT USER PAIN POINTS (WHERE OUR APP WOULD BE THE PERFECT FIT)

**1. LACK OF CONCRETE, ACTIONABLE ADVICE**

- Current Problem: Generic advice like 'workout harder in follicular, do yoga in luteal'
- User Quote: *"I feel like the apps I seen give pretty standard advice like during follicular work out harder, during luteal chill and do yoga - I'd like something that extends beyond that and can give concrete advice like: it's your luteal phase, you might crave sweet and fatty food, however this can lead to energy crashes. Food that might support you would be spinach (iron), fish (omega fats)"*
- **Our Opportunity:** Provide specific, science-backed nutrition and lifestyle recommendations with detailed explanations and rationale

**2. POOR ENERGY MANAGEMENT & PRODUCTIVITY OPTIMIZATION**

- Current Problem: Severe motivation and energy crashes affecting daily tasks
- User Quote: *"I get super demotivated a few days before my period - to the point where I can't even get simple tasks done"*
- **Our Opportunity:** Energy-optimized workload planning, productivity strategies, and concrete tips for maintaining motivation during low-energy phases

**3. INCONSISTENT TRACKING DUE TO POOR UX/ENGAGEMENT**

- Current Problem: Forgetting to log data, outdated design, poor reminder systems
- User Quote: *"I'm really bad at remembering dates, and as a busy Person, I often forgot to log my data. The app looks outdated from a design perspective, which made it less engaging. I always wanted notifications to remind me to log my data"*
- **Our Opportunity:** Intelligent reminder system, beautiful modern UI/UX, and gamification elements that make tracking enjoyable and habitual

**4. LACK OF SPECIALIZED HEALTH CONDITION SUPPORT**

- Current Problem: Apps don't account for PCOS, endometriosis, birth control effects
- User Quote: *"Would be nice if the app is adaptable based on user specific health cases such as PCOS, endometriosis etc. My cycle is so inconsistent due to birth control (IUD) that apps can't predict anything"*
- **Our Opportunity:** Personalized algorithms that adapt to individual health conditions, birth control types, and irregular cycles with specialized guidance

## KEY SKEPTICISM AREAS

**Generic Advice Frustration:**

- *"I feel like the apps I seen give pretty standard advice like during follicular work out harder, during luteal chill and do yoga - I'd like something that extends beyond that and can give concrete advice"*

**Medical Credibility Concerns:**

- Need for professional backing and clinical validation
- Users want evidence-based, specific recommendations
- *"Whether I'd pay really comes down to trust and accuracy. If an app is backed by actual medical professionals or has clinical validation, that's when I'd consider opening my wallet. Things like partnerships with gynecologists, or transparent methodology would definitely boost my confidence"*
- HOW WE COULD TACKLE THAT:
1. Partner with certified gynecologists and endocrinologists for content review
2. Conduct clinical trials to validate prediction accuracy
3. Display medical advisory board prominently in app
4. Provide peer-reviewed research citations for all recommendations
5. Offer transparent methodology explanations for algorithms
6. Get certifications from medical organizations or health authorities
7. Include disclaimers and clear scope of advice vs medical consultation

**App Switching Difficulty:**

- *"Changing from my Clue app would be difficult- there would have to be an easy and straightforward option to switch"*
- **HOW WE CAN TACKLE THAT:**
1. Build direct import tools for popular apps (Clue, Flo, Apple Health)
2. Offer manual data import via CSV/calendar uploads
3. Provide migration assistance through customer support
4. Create parallel usage period where users can test our app alongside existing ones
5. Offer data backup guarantee during transition period
6. Gradual feature introduction to ease learning curve
7. Video tutorials specifically for app switchers

**Privacy Invasion Concerns**

- User Quote: *"I track my cycle using a regular calendar and not an app because I feel like an app is invasive and would take away my privacy"*
- **HOW WE CAN TACKLE THAT:** Implement local-first data storage, transparent privacy policies, data export options, and optional cloud backup, No data selling policy with legal guarantees, Allow users to delete all data permanently

**Price Sensitivity**

- User Quote: *"I think as a student I have limited budget, but later on in my life, if the app predicts pretty well and give simple manageable tip that makes me feel good about myself, then I would pay 10€ monthly"*
- HOW WE CAN TACKLE THAT: Freemium model with robust free tier, graduated pricing (3-5€ premium, 8-10€ medical), student discounts

## Our 4 USER PERSONAS (Based on Response Patterns)

- Highest revenue potential (3-5€/month willingness)

- Time-constrained, productivity-focused
- Quote: *"concrete advice like: it's your luteal phase, you might crave sweet and fatty food, however this can lead to energy crashes. Food that might support you would be spinach (iron), fish (omega fats)"*

- Large volume, budget-conscious
- Prefers free solutions with privacy guarantees

**PERSONA 3: The Health-Struggling Seeker (25-40)**

- Willing to pay premium (5-10€/month) for effective solutions
- Severe symptoms affecting daily life
- Needs medical-grade advice and specialized support

- Already uses comprehensive health tracking
- Wants integration with existing fitness ecosystem
- Data-driven approach to optimization

## 6 STRATEGIC QUESTIONS FOR FURTHER APP DEVELOPMENT

Based on analysis of the survey responses, here are critical questions that need answers for optimal app development:

**1. How detailed should the onboarding questionnaire be to provide truly personalized advice?**

- Rationale:  Survey shows strong demand for personalized advice but no clarity on how much initial data users are willing to provide
- Options: a) Basic (age, cycle length, 2-3 main symptoms) b) Moderate (+ lifestyle, health conditions, goals) c) Comprehensive (+ detailed health history, preferences, medical info) d) Progressive (starts basic, builds over time)
- Why Important: Determines user onboarding experience and advice accuracy from day one

**2. What types and frequency of notifications would be most helpful without becoming intrusive?**

- Rationale: Survey mentions desire for reminders but no specific preferences on timing/content
- Options: a) Daily gentle reminders + phase transition alerts b) Personalized timing based on your schedule c) Only when significant predictions/recommendations available d) Customizable notification types and timing
- Why Important: Critical for user engagement and retention without causing notification fatigue

**3. Which existing app/device integrations would add the most value to your cycle tracking?**

- Rationale: Survey shows many users already use health apps but unclear which integrations are most valuable
- Options: a) Fitness trackers (Whoop, Fitbit, Apple Watch) b) Nutrition apps (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer) c) Calendar apps (Google, Apple, Outlook) d) Mental health/meditation apps (Headspace, Calm)
- Why Important: Helps prioritize development resources for maximum user value

**4. How do you prefer to receive educational content about hormonal health?**

- Rationale: Strong interest in learning but no clarity on preferred content format and depth
- Options: a) Daily bite-sized tips and facts b) Weekly in-depth articles with scientific backing c) Interactive lessons/courses you can complete d) Video content from medical professionals
- Why Important: Affects content strategy and user education engagement

**5. Would you be interested in any community or social features within the app?**

- Rationale: Survey doesn't address social/community aspects - unclear if users want connection or prefer privacy
- Options: a) Anonymous community forum for questions/support b) Ability to share data with healthcare provider c) Friend/family sharing for support partners d) Prefer completely private individual experience
- Why Important: Determines whether to invest in community features vs focusing on individual experience

**6. Which subscription model would you find most fair and appealing?**

- Rationale: Survey shows price sensitivity but limited options explored - need to validate optimal pricing structure
- Options: a) Freemium: Free basic features + €3-5/month premium b) Free trial: 30-day free trial then €5/month c) Tiered: Basic free + Premium €3/month + Medical €8/month d) One-time purchase: €30-50 for lifetime access
- Why Important: Critical for business model validation and revenue strategy</p>
